# underwater-acoustics

This work experimentally studies the propagation of acoustic waves in a water basin, using time-domain measurements acquired by a
set of sensors positioned at known locations. Three successive experiments are performed:

1. Estimation of the speed of sound in water: This first experiment consists of
determining the speed of sound propagation from the propagation times
measured between a fixed emitter and several receivers. The position of the source
emitting a signal with a well-defined peak over time is also
estimated using the spherical method.

2. Localization of a fixed acoustic emitter: The second experiment aims to
estimate the position of a fixed emitter, emitting a stationary or non-stationary signal,
using a hyperbolic localization method based on the
minimization of a least-squares criterion.

3. Localizing a Moving Transmitter: Finally, the third experiment
focuses on localizing a moving transmitter by analyzing the temporal evolution
of the received signals.
These three experiments employ signal processing tools, such as
cross-correlation for estimating time delays. The results obtained are
then compared to theoretical values ​​and known actual positions to assess
the accuracy and limitations of the methods used.
